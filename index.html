Who Are We.

Sunburst Communications Ltd. , founded and situated in Kenya, specializes in marketing & health communications, public relations development, event managment, advertising and graphic design, and is immensely proud of its Kenyan roots. Established in 2007, we provide our nationwide clients with an individual marketing service tailored to their particular requirements. Our philosophy demands that we set global standards for interactive communication and outstanding performance in the fields of brand architecture, communication, event management, marketing and publishing.

Our Process.

Our skills are of equal value to companies who depend on us to act as their marketing department as they are to those seeking to supplement existing resources – we work with both.

Our Approach.

Our work ethic is simple – We Listen! We Discuss! We Plan! We Execute! We deliver on the promise.

Our Goal.

We specialise in Creative Communication because we believe that creativity is the catalyst that energises every type of communications effort - whether for consumers, business customers, healthcare professionals, financial audiences, the media or employees. We contend that no communication is so important or so unimportant, so serious or so simple, that it can’t benefit from Sunburst’s creative thought and creative execution

Our Mission.

Sunburst Communications is committed to culturally sensitive strategic communications and providing excellent services and results for its clients.

Our Rockstar Team.

We are a mid-sized organisation, therefore we do not suffer from bureaucratic procedures inherent in large organistions. We make decisions faster thus are able to respond to your needs spontaneously. Many multinational organisations tend to reproduce strategies and messages packaged for specific audience in Europe and America. At Sunburst we have a rich knowledge of the local cultural context. We are able to develop and execute appropriate marketing and health communication strategies with impact for the Kenyan audience.
